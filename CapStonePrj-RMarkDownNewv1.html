Brief Description about the data set :

The purpose of EPA’s fuel economy estimates is to provide a reliable basis for comparing vehicles. Most vehicles in this guide (other than plug-in hybrids) have three fuel economy estimates: A city estimate, highway estimate and a combination of city and and highway driving. This data provides annual fuel cost estimates, rounded to the nearest $50 for each vehicle across different makes and models. The data also includes CO2 emissions, and smog ratings that helps evaluate environmental affects based on fuel consumption of vehicles.

The aim of the analysis is to understand the if fuel consumption is influenced by the other variables/elements in the dataset. And to understand if fuel efficient car lines have these variables at a higher proportion. Also to evaluate the environmental effects based on fuel consumption.

Data wrangling

Data wrangling was done for easier manipulation of the data. The data files are provided by year. Each individual data file is down loaded , saved as csv files to facilitate reading into data frames. The csv files are loaded into data frames. And in this step, the empty values of variables with some missing data are replaced with NA/None for easier data manipulation later. The column names have been renamed for easier understanding. ( Details of the wrangling can be found in data wrangling report https://github.com/royvel/IntroDatSciRprogSpringBoard/blob/master/DataWrangReport-CapstoneProj-RV.docx)

Exploratory and statistical Analysis

First, examined for distribution of different variables in the data set. Different plots (line, bar, histogram, box) were looked at and finally kept the plots that are easy to read and analyze. As a next step, studied the influence of various variables on fuel consumption (mpg). The key variables studied for this analysis are cylinders, engine displacement, transmission, and drive systems. There are other variables like viscosity, air aspiration methods, valves/valves timing etc in the data set. For the scope of this analysis, looked at variables that are usually looked by consumers while purchase of an vehicle. Examined the fuel consumption across different carlines and manufacturers. Correlation plots of key categorical variables was studied. Examined few scatter plots with liner regression to understand the correlation.

Finding correlations between different variables.

Key observations are cylinders having moderate -ve correlation with fuel consumption , i.e higher the number of cylinders, lower the fuel efficiency. So, cars with lower number of cylinders give more mile per gallon.

Cylinders and engine displacement show positive correlation. So, Engine displacement correlation to city fuel is similar to Cylinders. Higher the engine displacement, lower is the fuel efficiency.

city fuel and annual fuel cost show moderate to good -ve correlation. The fuel cost spent on cars with higher mile per gallon is lower than fuel cost spent on cars with lower miles per gallon.

City fuel and GHG rating show moderate positive correlation. Higher the mile per gallon, higher the GHG rating (lower the emissions). Higher GHG rating is better for the environment.

Summary of Data Analysis

The data analysis done so far shows the distribution of variables like cylinders, engine displacement, drive systems, transmission in the data set. Also within each of the carlines ,the distribution of these variables was studied. And the relation of these variables on the fuel efficiency was studied. Cylinders and engine displacement show negative correlation to fuel efficiency. Higher the number of cylinders, lower is the fuel efficiency. The linear regression line on scatter plot shows a downward slope indicating the same. The multiple variable linear model shows around 68% observed variance on the fuel effiency variable. Although not very significant, it is very likely that these variables do have an impact on fuel efficiency. Within the car lines, compact, midsize, small suv’s are most fuel efficient. Looking at distribution of cylinders within car lines, the fuel efficient car lines show higher proportion of 4 cylinders. Although statistical significance it not studied , correlation observed individually of cylinder and car line is in line with the distrution of cylinders within car lines. The GHG rating is high with higher fuel efficient cars. In summary, it can be prescribed that going with lower number of cylinders, low, engine displacement, automatic, 2 wheel drive cars will be more fuel efficient. Also, compact cars, midsize cars and small suv’s are better with low fuel usage and more environment safe. The analysis can be extended to understand the benefits on environment with the increase in more fuel efficienct cars. Also, can extend the analysis to understand the cost efficiency/savings these fuel efficient cars can have for consumers in the next few years.
